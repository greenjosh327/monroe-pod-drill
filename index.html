Monroe County POD Planning Drill

This prototype GIS planning product evaluates emergency food and water Point of Distribution (POD) coverage in Monroe County, Pennsylvania. The analysis uses Census block group demographics and two candidate POD locations to estimate population coverage and identify areas that may need additional distribution support.

Project Background

Following a disaster, counties may establish Points of Distribution to provide food, water, and emergency commodities to the public. This drill tests how GIS can support POD planning by estimating which populations are located within a reasonable planning distance of proposed POD sites.

The current analysis is based on two candidate POD locations:

  • Pleasant Valley High School Parking Lot
  • Urgent Care Parking Lot

Planning Results

  • Served Population: 126,781
  • Served Households: 46,246
  • Population Coverage: 75.68%
  • Unserved Population: 40,734
  • Unserved Households: 14,966
  • Unserved Population: 24.32%

Data and Method

  • 2024 ACS Census block group population and household data
  • Candidate POD site locations
  • 20-minute planning coverage model
  • 35 mph average travel speed assumption
  • Served and unserved population summarized by Census block group

Important Note

This is a planning and exercise product only. It is not an official operational deployment map. The current model uses a planning-distance assumption and should be refined with road-network drive-time analysis, local emergency management input, site access review, traffic flow planning, and commodity throughput estimates before operational use.

Next Steps
